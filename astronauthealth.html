Epigenetics

Andy Feinberg and his research group are studying the effects of space flight on the human body, using a set of twins: Scott and Mark Kelly. They will explore whether and how spending a year on the International Space Station changes an astronaut’s epigenetic markers — the reversible chemical modifications to a person’s DNA that affect how genes act on the body — over time.

Space Stethoscope

A team of students at the Johns Hopkins University’s Whiting School of Engineering has designed for NASA a new stethoscope that delivers accurate heart and body sounds to medics who are trying to assess astronauts’ health on long missions in noisy spacecraft.

People/Location:
James West & Team of undergraduates/Department of Mechanical Engineering

Precision Bone Loss Measurements

Bone loss and its strength-related implications are major health concerns for astronauts exposed to microgravity during long-duration spaceflight. A team of scientists led by APL’s Harry K. Charles Jr. investigates this issue.

People/Location:
Harry K. Charles Jr./APL

(More Info)

Sensorimotor Adaptation to Space Flight

A team in the department of Otolaryngology is gaining a better understanding of sensory processing for motor control in space, with an emphasis on adaptive capabilities and mathematical modeling.

People/Location:
Mark Shelhamer/Department of Otolaryngology-Head and Neck Surgery
