Astronaut Health

It wasn't too long ago that the first humans went to space, and Johns Hopkins researchers are determined to help astronauts continue to space travel as safely as possible!

  • All Works

Epigenetics

Andy Feinberg and his research group are studying the effects of space flight on the human body, using a set of twins. They will explore whether and how spending a year on the International Space Station changes an astronaut’s epigenetic markers — the reversible chemical modifications to a person’s DNA that affect how genes act on the body — over time.

Space Stethoscope

A team of students at the Johns Hopkins University’s Whiting School of Engineering has designed for NASA a new stethoscope that delivers accurate heart- and body-sounds to medics who are trying to assess astronauts’ health on long missions in noisy spacecraft.
