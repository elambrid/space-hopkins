Luciana Bianchi

Luciana Bianchi investigates the ultraviolet sky, the local group of galaxies, hot stars, and planetary nebulae. She was a co-Investigator of the NASA GALEX mission, and leads major science projects with the Hubble Space Telescope and ground-based telescopes.

William P. Blair

William Blair currently splits time between ongoing research projects at Johns Hopkins and as a planning system liaison scientist at the Space Telescope Science Institute, working on development activities for the James Webb Space Telescope project. He served as head of mission planning and as Chief of Observatory operations for the Far Ultraviolet Spectroscopic Explorer (FUSE) project at JHU. His primary research interest is in supernova remnants.

William Blair/Department of Physcis and Astronomy

Charles L. Bennett

Charles Bennett's research focus is on experimental/observational cosmology and instrumentation including space flight instrumentation. He served as the PI of the Wilkinson Microwave Anisotropy Probe mission, and is the CO-I of the Cosmology Large Angular Scale Surveyor (CLASS) telescopes.

People/Location:
Charles L. Bennett/Department of Physics and Astronomy

Probabilistic cross-identification of astronomy sources across observations, instruments and telescopes is at the heart of multi-spectral and time-domain analyses.

Sounding Rockets

Among the study of many other astronomical phenomena, sounding rockets observe the Earth's aurora, Venus, Jupiter, several comets including Halley and Hale-Bopp, the Io Plasma Torus, a Solar eclipse, several hot star calibrations, reflection and planetary nebulae, and the first Ultraviolet observation of quasar 3C273.

People/Location:
Stephan McCandliss; Paul D. Feldman/Department of Physics and Astronomy

Holland Ford

Holland Ford is a professor in the department and also an astronomer at the Space Telescope Science Institute. He is the principal investigator for the HST Advanced Camera for Surveys.

Riccardo Giacconi

Riccardo Giacconi is a professor in the Department of Physics and Astronomy of the Johns Hopkins University. His research is in experimental astrophysics. In 2002, he was awarded the Nobel Prize for Physics for his pioneering work inventing the field of X-ray astronomy.

People/Location:
Riccardo Giacconi/Department of Physics and Astronomy

(More Info)

Timothy Heckman

Heckman’s research has focused on the evolution of galaxies and supermassive black holes. He currently chairs the Pan-STARRS1 Board, is a member of the Committee on Astronomy & Astrophysics of the National Academy of Sciences, is a member of the Space Telescope Institute Council and the James Webb Space Telescope Advisory Committee, and serves as the Johns Hopkins representative on the Subaru Prime Focus Spectrograph Steering Committee.

People/Location:
Timothy Heckman/Department of Physics and Astronomy

(More Info)

Richard Conn Henry

Dick Henry joined the JHU Physics Department's faculty in 1968 as its first astronomer ever. Dr. Henry has more than two hundred publications, on topics including theoretical astrophysics, observational astronomy, radio astronomy, ultraviolet astronomy, and x-ray astronomy.

People/Location:
Richard Conn Henry/Department of Physics and Astronomy
