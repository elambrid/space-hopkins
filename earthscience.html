Global Ultraviolet Imager (GUVI)

GUVI is an instrument on the TIMED spacecraft designed to monitor the Earth's upper atmosphere. The GUVI science objectives are to understand the composition, energetics, and dynamics of the Earth's upper atmosphere

People: APL (More Info )

SuperMAG

SuperMAG is a worldwide collaboration of organizations and national agencies that currently operate more than 300 ground based magnetometers. The purpose of SuperMAG it to help scientists, teachers, students and the general public have easy access to measurements of the Earth's magnetic field.

People: APL (More Info )

Modeling atmospheric aerosol

Associate Research Scientist in the Department of Earth and Planetary Sciences of Johns Hopkins University and in the Laboratory for Atmospheric Chemistry and Dynamics of NASA Goddard Space Flight Center, Velentina Aquila focuses on modeling atmospheric aerosol and its impact on the climate.

People: Valentina Aquila (More Info)

Computer Models of atmospheric composition

Richard Stolarski is a Research Professor in the Department of Earth and Planetary Sciences heading several projects focusing on the chemical composition of the atmosphere; what governs the present composition; how does it respond to change and how did it evolve to the present state.

People: Richard Stolarski (More Info)

Large-scale dynamics and transport

Research projects focused on the dynamics and transport in the atmosphere, and its controls on the distribution of trace constituents. This is important for several different aspects of global change, including stratospheric ozone depletion, air pollution, and global warming.

People: Darryn Waugh (More Info)

Sounding Rockets

Among the study of many other astronomical phenomena, sounding rockets can be used to probe the Earth's upper atmosphere, the aurora and the airglow.

People: Paul D. Feldman (More Info)

Hydroclimate Research Group

Among the study of other hydroclimate processes, the hydroclimate research group aims to develop tools for improved monitoring and prediction of regional atmospheric processes.
