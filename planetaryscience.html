Sarah Horst

Investigating atmospheres located anywhere in time or space, including Titan, Europa, Exoplanets, and Early Earth!

People/Location: Sarah Horst/Department of Earth and Planetary Science (More Info)

Kevin Lewis

Research in planetary geophysics including the nature of sedimentary rocks of Mars, and what they might record about that planet's past climate and habitability. He is also interested in understanding the large-scale properties of planetary lithospheres using magnetic, gravity, and topography data sets.

People/Location: Kevin Lewis/Department of Earth and Planetary Science (More Info )

Peter L. Olson

Peter L. Olson and his team work to combine theory, numerical models, and laboratory fluid dynamics models, and use these to interpret global geophysical data pertaining to the deep interior of the Earth and other planets.

People/Location: Peter L. Olson/Department of Earth and Planetary Science (More Info)

BRRISON

Launch in 2013, BRRISON is a science balloon equipped to capture unique images and data from comet ISON as it passed through the inner solar system.

People: APL (More Info )

CONTOUR

Launched in 2002, The COmet Nucleus TOUR (CONTOUR) spacecraft was launched in 2002 to study two very different comets, Encke and Schwassmann-Wachmann-3, as they make periodic visits to our inner solar system.

People: APL (More Info)

Darrell Strobel

Darrell Strobel studies a broad range of fundamental problems in atmospheric chemistry, dynamics, and radiation pertinent to the atmospheres of the giant planets and their satellites with the goal to understand the global structure of composition, pressure, temperature, and winds.

People/Location: Darrell Strobel/Department of Earth and Planetary Science (More Info)

Harold Weaver

Hal Weaver pursues space-borne, rocket-borne, airborne, and ground-based investigations of planets, comets, and asteroids.

People/Location: Harold Weaver/Department of Physics and Astronomy (More Info)

New Horizons

New Horizons was launched in 2006, and is the first mission to investigate Pluto and its three moons. It will extend its mission by studying objects in the Kuiper Belt region.

People: APL (More Info)

Energetic Particle Detector

The Energetic Particle Detector (EPD) instrument onboard the Galileo spacecraft measured the characteristics of energetic particles to determine the size, shape, and dynamics of Jupiter's magnetosphere.

People: APL (More Info )

Europa Mission

APL and its partners are teamed to study a Europa Multiple Flyby Mission concept. This mission is a concept under study by NASA that would conduct detailed reconnaissance of Jupiter's moon Europa and would investigate whether the icy moon could harbor conditions suitable for life.

People: APL (More Info )

MESSANGER

The MErcury Surface, Space ENvironment, GEochemistry, and Ranging (MESSENGER) spacecraft is the first mission designed to orbit Mercury. The spacecraft completed its final Mercury flyby in 2009 and will enter Mercury orbit in March 2011 to carry out comprehensive measurements for one Earth year.

People: APL (More Info)

Magnetospheric IMaging Instrument

The Magnetospheric IMaging Instrument (MIMI) is an energetic particle detector onboard Cassini, a robotic spacecraft mission studying Saturn and its many natural satellites. The spacecraft launched in 1997 and entered into orbit around Saturn on June 30, 2004, after a long interplanetary voyage.

People: APL (More Info)

Compact Reconnaissance Imaging Spectrometer for Mars (CRISM)

Compact Reconnaissance Imaging Spectrometer for Mars (CRISM), a visible-infrared imaging spectrometer with scannable field of view flying aboard the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, is one of APL's advanced instruments seeking traces of past and present water on the martian surface.

People: APL (More Info)
